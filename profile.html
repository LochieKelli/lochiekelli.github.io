My Personal Profile

Online Personality Tests

There are hundreds, even thousands of different tests online which can help you determine many different things, whether its a psycopath test or a creativity test. Many employers ask their potential employees to do these tests to judge how they would work in a team.

I did three of these tests, the first one was the Myers-Briggs Personality Test from 16 Personalities, the second was a learning style test from EducationPlanner, and the second from TestMyCreativity.

Personality Test (Myers-Briggs)

From the Myers-Briggs Test, I got the result ENFP, which is the Campaigner personality. According to the site 16 Personalities, the Campaigner is a free spirit and independent character who enjoys the social and emotional connections they can obtain from others. Campaigners are curious, observant, and energetic. I feel like this would be useful when working in a team. I would be curious to listen to the other ideas which everyone has, observe how they think about different ideas and each other to decide what plan we should go with, and energetic/eager to get the project done. .

Learning Style (EducationPlanner)

According to EducationPlanner, I am a visual learner. A visual learner needs to visualize what they are learning to them remember. They may not understand or have difficulty understanding spoken directions. Visual learners find it easier to read maps and picture instructions compared to other learning styles.

Creativity Score (TestMyCreativity)

My creativity score, according to TestMyCreativity, is 39.28. Lower than the typical 63.09. According to the graph, I find it easier to make connections to things that don't have a visual reference and can theorise different concepts when it comes to ideas. My score is much higher in those two categories compared to the other six (all shown on the graph). The other six are ranked averagely the same, except for persistence, which is the ability to push through and come up with better solutions to problems or ideas, and perspective, which is the ability to see alternate solutions through different viewpoints.
